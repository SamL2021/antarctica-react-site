Day 10 ~ South Georgia, 20th March 2022

54o17'S, 036o30'W

Grytviken / Saint Andrews Bay

Weather: Clear skies in the morning then a bit cloudy

Sunrise 6:24 and sunset 18:45

Temp: 0 degrees to 2 degrees celsius

Today we visit Grytviken, the center of South Georgia's whaling industry and the only whaling station on the island that can be xplored. St Andrews Bay, meanwhile, is home to one of the world' biggest King penguin colonies. Estimated at approximately 300,000 penguins on our day of arrival

Visited Shakeltons grave, hiked into the South Georgia interior. Visited the whaling station, museam. Felt a fur seal and king penguin pelt, was chased by baby seals and send my nephews a post card from the end of the earth. Won the daily trivia with our 'Cool" crew team!

Day 11 ~ South Georgia, 21st March 2022

54o11'S, 036o35'W

Stromness / Jason Harbour

Weather: Cloudy and snowing

Sunrise 6:15 and sunset 18:45

Temp: 0 degrees to 2 degrees celsius

The official start of Autumn Equinox. Landed at Stromness in the morning, this is the whaling station that Ernest Shackleton, along with Frank Wilde and Tom Crean finally returned to civilisation after crossing the unexplored interior of South Georgia. The beach was covered in fur seals and the fields too, we walked up to a waterfall a couple of kms in where Shackleton and his men came down almost 106 years before. We won the daily trivia again! As we sailed for lunch we saw humpback whales lung feeding. In the afternoon we took a long, strenous hike over Jason Harbour in the afternoon to a viewpoint. We then enjoyed a soak in the jacuzzi before an early night in with room service and a movie.
